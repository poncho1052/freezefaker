# Freeze Faker Product Spec v0.1

## 1. Overview

### Title
Freeze Faker

### Tagline
Don’t Act Human.

### Genre
Observation hide-and-seek party game / asymmetric multiplayer / social deduction / stealth party game

### Core Concept
Freeze Faker is a competitive party game where human players hide among a crowd of NPCs. When the Red Light signal triggers, everyone freezes. The challenge is not only to stop moving, but to stop like an NPC.

Players can be caught not just because they move, but because their frozen posture, gaze, spacing, pose, or body language looks too human.

### One-line Pitch
NPCの群衆に紛れ、Red Lightでピタッと停止。動いていなくても、止まり方が人間っぽければバレる、観察型かくれんぼパーティーゲーム。

### English Pitch
Blend into an NPC crowd. Freeze when the Red Light hits. But don’t just stop — stop like an NPC.

## 3. Core Experience

### Faker Experience
Fakers must blend into the crowd while advancing toward goals.

Core feelings:

- I want to move, but I must move naturally.
- I want to freeze, but I must freeze like an NPC.
- I survived right in front of the Watcher.
- My disguise worked.

### Watcher Experience
Watchers must identify human-like inconsistencies in the crowd.

Core feelings:

- Something is off.
- That posture is too human.
- Is that a player or just an NPC?
- I caught them by reading body language.

### Viewer Experience
The game should be fun to watch even without playing.

Core viewer hook:

> この中に人間がいます。誰でしょう？

## 4. Game Modes

## 4.1 Classic: Freeze Run
The main mode.

Fakers start at one side of the map and must reach the goal while blending into NPC crowds. The Watcher observes during Red Light and accuses suspicious characters.

Recommended for:

- First-time players
- Streamers
- Short videos
- Public matchmaking

### Win Conditions
Faker win:

- At least one Faker reaches the goal

Watcher win:

- All Fakers are identified before they reach the goal

---

## 4.2 Mission: Blend Task
Fakers must complete several environmental tasks while blending into the crowd.

Example tasks:

- Look at a shop window
- Wait near a crosswalk
- Sit on a bench
- Check a vending machine
- Stand near a specific NPC
- Reach the station gate

Recommended for:

- Intermediate players
- More replayability
- Longer sessions

---

## 4.3 Custom Room
Players can create private rooms with custom rules.

Adjustable settings:

- Round length
- Green Light duration
- Red Light duration
- Number of Fakers
- NPC count
- Watcher mark count
- False accusation penalty
- Map selection
- Mission settings
- NPC Sync on/off
- Camera settings

Recommended for:

- Streamers
- Friend groups
- Community events

---

## 4.4 Tutorial / Solo Practice
A single-player practice mode to learn core mechanics.

Tutorial topics:

- Green / Red Light rhythm
- Faker movement
- Freezing naturally
- NPC Sync
- Disguise actions
- Watcher accusation
- Reading suspicious behavior

## 7. Core Systems

### Green / Red Light
The game alternates between movement and freeze phases.

Green Light:

- Fakers can move freely
- NPCs move naturally
- Watcher observes but cannot accuse by default

Red Light:

- NPCs freeze naturally
- Fakers must stop and appear natural
- Watcher can accuse suspicious characters

### Red Light Warning
A short warning before Red Light.

Examples:

- Signal blink
- Audio cue
- BGM drop
- Public announcement
- Subtle screen warning

Recommended warning length:

- 0.8〜1.5 seconds

---

## 8. Differentiation

Freeze Faker is not just Red Light Green Light.

Difference:

- Traditional Red Light Green Light: if you move, you lose.
- Freeze Faker: even if you stop, you lose if you stop like a human.

Freeze Faker is not just NPC hide-and-seek.

Difference:

- Traditional NPC hide-and-seek: blend while walking or doing tasks.
- Freeze Faker: the decisive moment is the frozen pose, gaze, spacing, and posture.

Core differentiation:

> 動いたかどうかではなく、人間っぽく見えるかどうかを見抜くゲーム。

## 12. Success Criteria

Before commercial release, the game should satisfy:

- New users understand the rule within 10 seconds
- One round creates at least 2 funny or surprising moments
- 30 minutes of play can produce at least 5 short-video moments
- Both Faker and Watcher roles are fun
- Bad players are still entertaining
- False accusations create laughter, not only frustration
- Red Light freeze moments are visually satisfying
- Stream viewers can participate by guessing
- Players naturally say “one more round”
