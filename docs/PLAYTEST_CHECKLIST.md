# Freeze Faker Playtest Checklist

Use this checklist to evaluate early builds. The goal is not polish. The goal is to verify whether the core idea is fun and understandable.

---

## 1. Test Setup

### Recommended Group
- 3〜6 players if multiplayer exists
- If local-only, one tester operates Faker and another observes as Watcher

### Test Length
- 30〜60 minutes

### Required Build Features
- NPCs move and freeze
- Faker can move toward goal
- Watcher can accuse
- Round can end

---

## 2. Pre-Test Questions

Ask before explaining too much:

1. Looking at the screen, what do you think this game is about?
2. What do you think Red Light means?
3. Who do you think is suspicious in the crowd?

Purpose:

- Confirm whether the game is visually understandable.

---

## 3. Core Understanding

After one round, ask:

1. Did you understand the rule within 10 seconds?
2. Did you understand what Faker is trying to do?
3. Did you understand what Watcher is trying to do?
4. Did Red Light / Green Light feel clear?
5. Did the win condition feel clear?

Pass condition:

- Most testers understand the basic loop after one round.

---

## 4. Faker Experience

Ask Faker players:

1. Did you feel like you were blending into the crowd?
2. Did Red Light feel tense?
3. Did you try to stop like an NPC?
4. Did you know why you were caught?
5. Did you feel you had meaningful choices?
6. Did you want to play another round?

Observe:

- Do Fakers naturally slow down before Red Light?
- Do Fakers look for natural stopping spots?
- Do Fakers use NPCs as cover?
- Do Fakers laugh when caught?

---

## 5. Watcher Experience

Ask Watcher players:

1. Did you feel you could detect suspicious behavior?
2. Did you have enough time during Red Light?
3. Were false accusations funny or frustrating?
4. Did the crowd feel readable?
5. Was it fun to inspect posture, gaze, and spacing?
6. Did you want another chance as Watcher?

Observe:

- Does Watcher scan the crowd naturally?
- Does Watcher verbalize suspicion?
- Does Watcher make false accusations?
- Does Watcher learn over multiple rounds?

---

## 6. Viewer / Stream Test

Show a 10〜30 second clip to someone who did not play.

Ask:

1. Can you tell what is happening?
2. Can you guess who the human is?
3. Would you comment your guess?
4. Would this make a good short video?
5. Is it fun to watch even without playing?

Pass condition:

- Viewers can participate by guessing.

---

## 7. Red Light Moment

Evaluate:

- Does the crowd freeze moment look satisfying?
- Is it clear when Red Light starts?
- Does the freeze create tension?
- Are suspicious poses visible?
- Is the scene too visually noisy?

Score 1〜5:

- Visual satisfaction
- Clarity
- Tension
- Comedy
- Readability

---

## 8. Fun Moment Count

During a 30-minute test, count:

- Funny false accusations
- Close survival moments
- Obvious suspicious poses
- Missed Faker moments
- Last-second goal moments
- Moments worth clipping

Target:

- 5+ clip-worthy moments in 30 minutes

---

## 9. Balance Checks

### Watcher too strong if:
- Fakers are caught immediately every round
- Red Light gives too much inspection time
- NPC behavior is too regular
- Faker movement stands out too much

### Watcher too weak if:
- Fakers always win easily
- Crowd is too dense
- Fakers look identical to NPCs
- Red Light is too short
- There are too many false positives

### Faker too boring if:
- There is nothing to do except walk
- No meaningful stopping choices exist
- Goal is too close or too far
- Red Light feels random and unfair

---

## 10. Must-Fix Signals

Prioritize fixes if testers say:

- “I don’t know what I’m supposed to do.”
- “I can’t tell NPCs and players apart at all.”
- “I can always tell who the player is.”
- “Red Light feels unfair.”
- “Watcher is boring.”
- “Faker is boring.”
- “I wouldn’t play another round.”
- “This looks like a generic NPC hide-and-seek game.”

---

## 11. Success Signals

The concept is working if testers say:

- “Wait, was that a player?”
- “That pose is suspicious.”
- “I was sure that was human.”
- “How did you not see me?”
- “One more round.”
- “This would be funny on stream.”
- “I want to try Watcher.”
- “I want to try Faker.”
